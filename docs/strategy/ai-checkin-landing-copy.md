## 1. Headline + subhead

**Option A — _recommended_**

> # Your AI rollout isn't stalling on the tech. It's stalling on the people.
>
> ### A free, ~20-minute team check-in that surfaces what your people actually think about AI — before it quietly decides for them.

_Why recommended: leads with the human problem (the issue's stated angle), names the real blocker, and the subhead carries all three proof points — free, fast, honest. Evergreen; no buzzword expiry date._

**Option B — conversation-forward**

> # Everyone's using AI. Almost no one's talking about it.
>
> ### Run an honest, ~20-minute team check-in. Free, works for distributed and multilingual teams.

**Option C — direct / outcome-led**

> # Start the AI conversation your team is already having without you.
>
> ### A simple, ~20-minute check-in to turn quiet anxiety into shared direction. No tools to install. Free.

## 2. Value props (3)

Use as a 3-up row beneath the hero. Title + one supporting line each.

**An honest conversation, not a survey**
People won't put their real AI fears in a feedback form. They'll say them out loud when it's safe to. The check-in creates that room — and gets the quiet stuff on the table.

**About 20 minutes. That's the whole ask.**
No workshop, no consultant, no slide deck to prep. Draw a card, ask the question, listen. One team meeting is all it takes to start.

**Free, and built for real teams**
Works in person or remote, across languages and time zones. No login, no install, nothing to buy. Just open the deck and begin.

## 6. On-brand notes for the builder

- Keep WhoCards' warmth: short sentences, second person, "start the conversation," "what's true for your team." Avoid enterprise jargon ("synergy," "transformation journey," "unlock value").
- The product is the deck. AI is the topic, never the pitch. Never imply WhoCards is an AI product.
- Stats are load-bearing for credibility — keep every source link live. Refresh annually so the page stays evergreen.
